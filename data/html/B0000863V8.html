    Not Bad.
    By Nancy Price on May 17, 2012
    Size: 42KColor: Black Verified Purchase
    This bra seems comfortable. I just received it, so I have yet to see how it will feel after several washings in a month or so. Perhaps I can update then.

    It has the typical feel of Aviana bras that I recall from having worn them many years ago. So they haven't changed much in terms of construction.

    This bra has a somewhat stretchy feel in the back, while being very strongly structured in the side panels. That results in a very firm, supportive feeling while wearing the bra.

    The cups themselves have the usual Aviana slightly odd cup shape. This one makes an attempt at being a little more rounded, but that is ruined by the support slings that are inside the cup, resulting in a flattened effect across the cleavage. Not sure how that will look under clothes, but it has a very distinct flat area that results in a dented appearance.

    The straps almost seem like an afterthought. They don't provide the support- the side panels and inner slings do all the heavy lifting. They are stretchy, comfortable and unnoticeable.

    One warning, if you are a woman who puts bras on at the waist and pulls them up, the Aviana bras are somewhat difficult to get into. The straps and side-panels fit very snugly, requiring some yoga-like ability to get your arms through.

    The pattern and fabric is nice. It is solid and not see-through. Very satiny too.

    This bra fit me a little largely, but I probably needed a J cup instead of the K that I bought. I had to guesstimate my cup size. I would say based on the feel that this bra runs pretty true to size.

    Overall, it's an okay bra. Not a *wonderful* bra, but I won't be returning it, despite it being a size larger than I needed (my fault, see above). It's comfortable enough, and shouldn't wind up in my not-gonna-wear-it pile.

    By Mimifly16 on August 16, 2015
    Size: 46JColor: Candlelight Verified Purchase
    I really like this bra! It has good support & great coverage in both the cleavage & arm pit areas to prevent me from spilling out. I was fitted for it in a bra store about a year ago & was given a 40H. I have since gained some weight & it was getting too small as well as worn out. Since the bra store I had previously been fitted at is located about 2 hours from where I live, I decided to try to figure out the size I needed on my own. I measured 52" below my arm pits & 60" across my bust. According to the chart I should order a 52H! I knew this couldn't possibly be right! There was no way the bra I was currently wearing was 6 SIZES TOO SMALL! I figured I needed to go up possibly 2-3 band sizes, so I ordered a 44K & a 46J. The bra was not available in a 44H at the time I placed my order, so I tried to adjust band sizes & cups. When they arrived, the 44K seemed too small at the band; although, now looking back, I think it might've just been different than the feel of the old worn out 40H. :-) The 46J seemed to fit well at the band, but the cups were a little too big. As I was desperate for a better fitting bra than the old worn out 40H, I decided to keep the 46J & return the 44K. After the first day of wearing the 46J, I realized the band size was too big after all, but it's still better than what I had been wearing. So I'm still not sure what size I really need, perhaps I'll try again to get a 44H. Once I figure it out though, I'll be good to go!
